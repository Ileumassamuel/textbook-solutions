1.  
$$
\begin{aligned}
4 - x & < 3- 2x \\
4 + x & < 3 \\
x & < -1
\end{aligned}
$$


2.  
$$
\begin{aligned}
5 - x^2 & < 8 \\
5 & < 8 + x^2 \\
-3 & < x^2
\end{aligned}
$$
Notice that
$\forall{x} \in \mathbb{R}, x^2 > 0$. Thus, any real number would
satisfy this equation.

3.  
$$
\begin{aligned}
5 - x^2 & < - 2 \\
5 & < -2 + x^2 \\
7 & < x^2 \\
x < -\sqrt{7} \quad \vee \quad x > \sqrt{7}
\end{aligned}
$$


4.  
$$
\begin{aligned}
(x - 1)(x - 3) > 0 & \implies 
(x - 1 > 0 \wedge x - 3 > 0) \\ 
& \quad \vee
\; \; (x - 1 < 0 \wedge x - 3 < 0)
\end{aligned}
$$
This is so since: 
$$
\begin{aligned}
\forall{x} \in \mathbb{R}, x > 0, \exists{y, z}: x = yz
\implies [ y > 0 \wedge z > 0 ] \vee
[ y < 0 \wedge z < 0]
\end{aligned}
$$
