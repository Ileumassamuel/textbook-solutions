$$
\begin{aligned}
n_{0} \in A & \implies n_{0} + 1 \in A \\
& \implies (n_{0} + 1) + 1 \in A \\
& \implies (n_{0} + 2) + 1 \in A \\
& \qquad \dots
\end{aligned}
$$


Thus, A is defined just as the natural numbers except that its first
term is $n_{0}$ instead of 0.
